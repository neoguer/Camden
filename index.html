About

Camden Archambeau is a cellist based in New York City. Recent appearances include concerts at Carnegie Hall, Jordan Hall, Steinway Hall, the Neue Galerie, Millbrook Music Salon, and Avaloch Farm.

Passionate about creative programming, he has presented recital series at the Metropolitan Opera Club and Signet Society. In the 2023-24 season, he served as Artist-in-Residence at Lowell House and received the St. Botolph Club Emerging Artist Award and the Robert Levin Prize for his work as a cellist and conductor.

His has completed residencies at Music@Menlo, Avaloch Farm, the Stauffer Center, the Perlman Music Program, and Bowdoin Festival Fellowship Program. Camden has served as Principal Cellist of the Juilliard Orchestra, New York String Orchestra, and Schleswig-Holstein Festival Orchestra. As a member of the Archambeau-Dastoor Duo, he will appear at the Boston Museum of Fine Arts, Winter Harbor Music Festival, and Avaloch Farm this season.

Camden is an assistant conductor and chamber music coach at Boston Youth Symphony Orchestras. He has appeared as a guest conductor with the Harvard-Radcliffe Orchestra and was the director of Harvard's Bach Society Orchestra. He holds a B.A. in History from Harvard and previously studied with Joel Krosnick, Paul Katz, Sieun Lin, and Adam Grabois. He studies with David Finckel and Nina Lee at Juilliard.
